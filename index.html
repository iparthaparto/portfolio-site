JioCinema (now JioHotstar) — IPL 2023 & 2024

JioCinema was India's leading OTT streaming platform and the official digital broadcaster of the Tata IPL — the world's most-watched cricket league. The platform has since merged with Disney+ Hotstar under the JioStar joint venture to become JioHotstar, India's largest streaming service with a 500M+ user base. Across two consecutive IPL seasons, the mandate spanned both B2C performance marketing to drive viewer acquisition and B2B communication to engage advertising partners across one of the most competitive content windows in Indian media.

Objective

Power JioCinema's IPL marketing engine across two full seasons — turning the world's biggest cricket league into a daily acquisition and engagement opportunity. Drive app installs, Connected TV viewership, and watch-time through every stage of each season, while building B2B credibility with advertisers and partners through polished, regionally tuned campaign communication.

Campaign Approach

The work spanned three connected layers across both seasons. Performance creative was built for scale and speed — OEM and CTV adapts, match-specific design templates, and partnership integrations like My11Circle's Jeeto Dhan Dhana Dhan and RCB Playbook, each tuned to platform, format, and fan moment. B2B communication carried into digital and print formats with regional-language adapts (Gujarati, Marathi, Hindi and more) to bring advertisers into the IPL conversation across India's heartland markets.

Campaign film production translated each season's energy into culturally rooted storytelling, with on-ground sets, ensemble casts, and a tone built for India's heartland audience. In 2024, this expanded further with a holistic influencer shoot for the #ViralWeekends programme on JioCinema, produced in partnership with TSBI home production — a multi-talent shoot designed to bring creators, comedians, and cultural voices into the IPL conversation, expanding the platform's appeal beyond traditional cricket viewership.

Amplification

Across each season, the campaign delivered 6,000+ performance creatives and 1,500+ video assets — a season-long content engine running at IPL pace across every partner format. In 2023, the work helped drive Connected TV viewership to 2× HD Linear TV and lifted ad-spot sales by 20%, establishing JioCinema as a serious challenger in India's premium broadcast market.

In 2024, the #ViralWeekends influencer programme drew 50M+ views once the TSBI-produced shoots aired across JioCinema's official platforms, broadening the conversation around IPL beyond cricket's traditional audience. B2B print and partner-facing communication carried the campaign into advertiser conversations and trade environments across both years, positioning JioCinema as the definitive home of IPL — built for performance, scaled for partnership, and shaped for the cultural moment cricket creates every summer.

Objective

Push viewership for Colors Cineplex through the RSWS window by turning social into a steady feeder for the broadcast. JioCinema (now JioHotstar) — FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022

JioCinema was the official digital broadcaster of the FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022 in India, streaming the tournament free-to-watch for the first time on Indian OTT. The platform has since merged with Disney+ Hotstar under the JioStar joint venture to become JioHotstar. The brief covered the influencer-led layer of the campaign — designed to take the tournament beyond traditional football fans and into the wider Indian cultural conversation.

Objective

Pull a non-football audience into the World Cup window through a campaign that travelled on sound and emotion rather than match schedules. Give Indian viewers a reason to feel part of the tournament from home, and turn the buzz into tune-ins for JioCinema's free-to-stream broadcast.

Campaign Approach

The campaign was built around an original anthem — "Aap Qatar mein hai" (You're in Qatar) — written to make Indian fans feel transported to the host nation, even if they were watching from their living rooms. Music influencers across India were invited to create their own versions of the song, reinterpreting it in their own languages, styles, and visual settings. The hook was simple enough to travel, but distinctive enough to stay tied to JioCinema's World Cup window.

Strategy · Own Venture · MSc Dissertation

Human In Sports (HIS)

Human In Sports is a digital-first sports media venture developed through my MSc dissertation. The platform is built to address a structural gap in global sports media: less than 5% of mainstream coverage is devoted to women, para-athletes, or athletes in non-traditional sports, despite rising participation across all three categories.

Context

The idea was shaped by five years of working inside the sports industry and observing the same pattern across markets. Athletes representing their countries in futsal, handball, athletics, and para-sports operate with limited facilities, minimal sponsorship, and almost no media coverage, while mainstream cricket and football continue to absorb the majority of attention and investment.

Project Scope

The business plan covers nine chapters across two sections.

Section one defines the venture: mission, vision, services, target customers, and unique selling proposition. It is supported by primary research conducted through a quantitative survey of 76 respondents aged 18 to 35, combining athletes, fans, and digital-first sports consumers.

Section two builds the operational and financial roadmap: a five-year marketing strategy, a complete revenue model, cash flow forecasting, break-even analysis, balance sheet projections, and risk management.

Strategic Frameworks Applied
  • PESTLE Analysis to map the external macro environment across political, economic, social, technological, legal, and environmental factors.
  • Porter's Five Forces to assess competitive pressure within the sports media industry.
  • Blue Ocean Strategy to position HIS in uncontested market space rather than competing directly with legacy broadcasters or established OTT platforms.
  • Ansoff Matrix to plan growth across market penetration, product development, geographic expansion, and diversification.
  • 7Ps Marketing Mix and Chaffey's RACE Framework to translate brand positioning into an integrated marketing communications strategy across Instagram, YouTube, and Spotify.
  • Keller's Brand Equity Model and Booms and Bitner's Service Marketing Model to define product and audience experience.
Financial Model

The five-year financial plan uses a blended funding structure: angel investment, innovation grants, sponsorship-based equity, and contra agreements, designed to reduce founder equity dilution and dependency on any single revenue source.

Revenue is modelled across five streams: subscriptions, sponsorships, merchandise, events, and grants. The plan projects a deliberate Year 1 deficit driven by content and marketing investment, with break-even reached in Year 3 and sustained profitability from Year 4 onwards.
